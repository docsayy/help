Stroke & Brain Imaging

What each imaging study tells you and how it guides acute stroke decisions

Purpose of This Guide

This page is a rapid review for understanding why specific brain imaging is ordered in stroke and what clinical decision each study unlocks. It is designed for internal medicine residents working in stroke centers and community hospitals.

Imaging Modalities

CT Head (Non-Contrast)

What it answers:

  • Is there intracranial hemorrhage?
  • Is there a large established infarct?

What it does NOT do:

  • Does not rule out early ischemic stroke
  • Poor sensitivity for posterior circulation stroke

Clinical role: Determines safety of thrombolysis.

A normal CT head does not mean there is no stroke.
CTA Head & Neck

What it answers:

  • Is there a large vessel occlusion (LVO)?
  • Is there arterial dissection or critical stenosis?

Key vessels to recognize:

  • Internal carotid artery (ICA)
  • MCA M1 / proximal M2
  • Basilar artery
  • Vertebral arteries
  • PCA P1

Clinical role: Determines thrombectomy eligibility and need for transfer.

CTA frequently changes management even when CT head is normal.
CT Perfusion

What it answers:

  • How much brain is already infarcted (core)?
  • How much brain is salvageable (penumbra)?

Conceptual interpretation:

  • Low blood flow + low blood volume → infarct core
  • Delayed flow with preserved volume → penumbra

Clinical role: Extends thrombectomy window (6–24 hours).

Modern stroke care treats tissue, not just the clock.
MRI DWI (Diffusion-Weighted Imaging)

What it answers:

  • Has acute infarction occurred?

Key concept:

  • Restricted diffusion = infarcted tissue
  • Becomes positive within minutes of ischemia

Clinical role: Confirms acute ischemic stroke and differentiates mimics.

MRI FLAIR

What it answers:

  • How old is the infarct?

Key concept:

  • FLAIR positive → older injury
  • FLAIR negative → recent stroke
DWI–FLAIR Mismatch

Meaning:

  • DWI positive with FLAIR negative suggests recent stroke

Clinical role: Allows thrombolysis in wake-up or unknown-onset strokes.

Key Takeaways

  • CT → Is there blood?
  • CTA → Is there a blocked artery?
  • Perfusion / MRI → Is brain dead or salvageable?
