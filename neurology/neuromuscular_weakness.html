Neuromuscular Weakness & Respiratory Failure

Pattern recognition, localization, and when to intubate early

Home Back to Neurology

Core Principle

Neuromuscular weakness causes ventilatory failure before hypoxia. Waiting for low O₂ or high CO₂ is waiting too long.

Golden rule: If you’re worried about breathing mechanics, check NIF and FVC — not just ABG.

Step 1: Localize the Weakness Pattern

A Ascending vs Descending
Ascending
Legs → arms → bulbar/respiratory → think GBS.
Descending
Cranial nerves → arms → legs → think botulism, MG.
B Fatigable vs Fixed
Fatigable
Worse with use, improves with rest → myasthenia gravis.
Fixed/progressive
Steady worsening → neuropathy, myopathy, cord.
C UMN vs LMN
UMN signs
Hyperreflexia, spasticity → brain/spinal cord.
LMN signs
Hyporeflexia, atrophy, fasciculations → nerve, NMJ, muscle.

Step 2: Respiratory Red Flags ICU-critical

  • Use of accessory muscles, weak cough
  • Inability to count to 20 in one breath
  • Pooling secretions / dysphagia
  • Orthopnea or paradoxical breathing
Numbers Bedside Respiratory Metrics
NIF
> −30 cmH₂O reassuring; < −20 concerning.
FVC
< 20 mL/kg → high risk of respiratory failure.
Trend
Rapid decline matters more than a single value.
Do not wait for hypoxia or hypercapnia to intubate.

High-Yield Syndromes

GBS Guillain–Barré Syndrome
Pattern
Ascending symmetric weakness + hyporeflexia.
Danger
Autonomic instability, respiratory failure.
Diagnostics
LP: albuminocytologic dissociation; EMG later.
Treatment
IVIG or plasmapheresis (NOT steroids).
Frequent NIF/FVC checks save lives in GBS.
MG Myasthenia Gravis Crisis
Pattern
Fatigable weakness, ptosis, diplopia, bulbar symptoms.
Triggers
Infection, surgery, steroids, certain meds.
Treatment
IVIG or plasmapheresis; hold acetylcholinesterase inhibitors if intubated.
Avoid: magnesium, fluoroquinolones, aminoglycosides.
Botulism
Pattern
Descending paralysis, dilated pupils, autonomic symptoms.
Clue
GI prodrome or food exposure.
Action
Call public health early; antitoxin + ICU.
Cord Cervical Spinal Cord Compression
Pattern
Weakness below level ± sensory level ± bladder involvement.
Action
Emergent MRI spine.

What to Order (Targeted)

  • NIF / FVC trending
  • MRI brain/spine if UMN signs or sensory level
  • LP if GBS suspected
  • Autoantibodies if MG suspected (do not delay treatment)

When to Call Neurology

  • Any rapidly progressive weakness
  • Declining respiratory metrics
  • Suspected GBS, MG crisis, or botulism
  • Unclear localization or need for immunotherapy
